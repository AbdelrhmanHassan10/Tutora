About the Grand Egyptian Museum

A Journey Through Time: The Story of the GEM

The Grand Egyptian Museum (GEM) is the culmination of a vision to create a world-class institution dedicated to the preservation and display of Egypt's rich Pharaonic heritage.

visibility

The Vision

Early 2000s

The idea for a new, modern museum to house Egypt's vast collection of ancient artifacts was born, aiming to provide a worthy home for treasures like those of Tutankhamun.

account_balance

Foundation Stone

2002

The foundation stone of the Grand Egyptian Museum was officially laid by President Hosni Mubarak, marking the dawn of the most ambitious cultural project of the 21st century.

architecture

International Design Competition

2003

An international competition was held in 2003 to select an architectural design. The winning entry by Heneghan Peng Architects was chosen for its modern interpretation of ancient Egyptian themes.

settings

Construction Begins

2005

The monumental task of building the world's largest archaeological museum began in 2005 on the Giza plateau, overlooking the majestic pyramids.

science

Conservation Excellence

2010

The GEM Conservation Center officially opened in 2010. It is one of the largest and most advanced in the world, preserving over 50,000 artifacts.

workspace_premium

Ramses II's Arrival

2018

The 3,200-year-old colossus of Ramses II was moved to its permanent home in the museum's grand entrance hall in a spectacular engineering feat.

directions_boat

The Solar Boat's Journey

2021

The ancient Khufu Solar Boat was painstakingly transported from its site near the Great Pyramid to its dedicated hall within the GEM.

inventory_2

Tutankhamun's Treasures Arrive

2022

The meticulous transfer of over 5,000 artifacts from the tomb of the Golden King Tutankhamun was completed, preparing them for their first-ever unified display.

key

Trial Operations

2024

The museum launched limited trial tours, inviting the world to experience the majestic Grand Staircase and the commercial district for the first time.

auto_awesome

The Grand Opening

2026

A historic celebration marks the official inauguration of the world's largest archaeological museum, housing the full collection of King Tutankhamun.

A Modern Marvel: The Architectural Splendor of the GEM

The museum's design is a testament to architectural innovation, blending seamlessly with its historic surroundings while making a bold contemporary statement.

The Grand Staircase

Ascend through the history of ancient Egypt on our monumental Grand Staircase. Spanning four levels, this architectural centerpiece showcases the chronological progression of Pharaonic art, leading you towards the breathtaking view of the Pyramids.

  • 64 meters of panoramic history
  • 87 statues and architectural fragments
  • 4 distinct eras from Old Kingdom to Late Period
