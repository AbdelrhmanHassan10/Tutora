18th Dynasty • New Kingdom

Golden Mask of Tutankhamun

The Boy King

Sealed within the Valley of the Kings for over three millennia, this funerary mask belonged to the boy king Tutankhamun. Discovered in 1922, it remains the ultimate symbol of ancient Egyptian artistry and the quest for immortality.

Symbolism & Mastery

The mask consists of two sheets of gold that were hammered together and weigh over 10 kilograms. The obsidian and quartz eyes were designed to catch the light, ensuring the Pharaoh could see through the darkness of the afterlife.

Height 54 cm
Gold Purity 22.5 Karat

The Discovery

"As my eyes grew accustomed to the light, details of the room within emerged slowly from the mist... gold—everywhere the glint of gold."

— Howard Carter, November 1922
